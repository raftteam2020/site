INTRODUCTION TO AI: 

With the rise with both demand and dependency upon AI, clear, structured and motivating introduction to this very peculiar area is crucial for enough young students to feel comfortable and confident. With the right amount of partnerships and collaborations with various industries and professionals, prg.ai will determine the path that Czech student community will take in the next following years and so on. 




“On an annual basis, the rise within internet users is rising from 24% to 32%. From 4% web users in 2010 we are currently facing 58% and nearly over 95% of usage in the age group of 16-24”. 

Marketing Strategies and 
Procedures

  • Inform and Educate
  • Introduce Industry-Specific Work
  • Practical Implementation

The very specific and complex base of working with AI can scare off beginners that would otherwise have a very successful career. The lack of access to knowledge and information about this area of our human technological development therefore creates so called social anxiety that strictly eliminates spontaneous enthusiasm and imposes a barrier for newcomers. To elaborate on this unpleasant reality we will point out the following: In all the PR material, we think it would be beneficial to show a realistic picture of working in IT including unpleasant experiences such as gender pay gap, sexual harassment, prejudices towards women etc. We believe that this way it would be more credible than sharing only the nice things. 

To close the gap between the professionals and amateurs, specific channels and interactive platforms are needed to be implemented:

  • podcasts
  • webinars
  • social media platforms

The PR materials should be a good combination of all the possible views on the job of IT people. That means, apart from traditional information about the job, we should include:

  • Fuckupnights
  • Science Slams

Each of these areas will require a speaker from various firms representing the following and enabling the opportunity to gain various insights and background information in the form of the above mentioned forms, and vice versa students will gain the opportunity to visit chosen workplaces.

Healthcare: opportunities for medical student to seek alternatives and becoming more efficient in the combination of AI and medicine.

Education: with the rise of social constraints (largely affected by current pandemic situation), remote teaching will gain much more significant portion of a normalized teaching method.

Marketing: spreading a message via web and social platforms, AI will be crucial to attract large amounts of people by simulating real life experiences.

Retail and Sales: with the rise of the variability of goods produces in our market, the saturation of each industry will force many more companies to come up with completely differentiated st:ategies in order to increase their revenues and become significant competitors in the market.

Financial markets and services: AI will allow professionals in the financial sectors to be more precise and predict trends extended much into the long term aspect in the future, therefore the simple application of AI will not replace any labour force, on the other hand, it will allow each firm to even extend their reliance on machine precision to customers.

Heavy industries: 
Within the heavy industries, AI is not only capable of ensuring a smoother and much more effective transition between the collaboration between human intelligence and force and the power of man made machines. 


Communication: With the rise of the trend of frequent employment changes for the current generation, flexibility and a clear organization will be absolutely crucial. AI will therefore not only improve communication and scheduling of professional matters but also sort out ideas, thoughts and sources of information.

Social connections: This aspect is already reaching its full peak with AI being used to its fullest, distances between people will remain solely at the base of physical constraints. 

Web searching: As much as the rise of information is great, the spread of malformation and intrusive individuals increases every day. For that reason, AI will enable much quicker and safer detection of malusage of one's own private space online. 

Goods and services: With e-commerce and retail shifting to an online environment, much of personal data and financially related matters will be needed to be incorporated into personal devices and used as effectively as possible. Furthermore, data that has been thought of as only meant for paper purposes will be needed to be available online as the current trends show a much bigger trust to social and web relations between people and electronically insurance of data gains a trustworthy reputation. With the help of AI techniques, medical, financial and personal records will be kept on devices and in a safe storage online for further usage. 

Building an AI Community

  • About
  • Education
  • Networking

For potential students, it is very important to see that other studying IT means meeting a lot of inspirational people who are willing to share their experience, help to overcome troubles and last but not least, have fun together. Therefore, it is necessary to build a community facilitated by prg.ai, which will consist of students, young professionals and university professors. 




A good example of a successful IT community is Studentské tréninkové centrum (STC) by Microsoft. (https://www.microsoft.com/cs-cz/education/students/stc) The crucial difference is that the new community will not be exclusively bound to a single company, university or town. Other good examples if IT communities are https://www.czechitas.cz/en/ or slovakian https://www.ajtyvit.sk/. Their only problem is that they are of small scale. 


Educational workshops in all possible forms:

  • Summer school
  • Weekend courses
  • Online sessions
  • One-day seminars
  • Competitions

One important activity is IT competitions. There are multiple ways to approach them. First, we can create new ones which will be related to activities performed as a part of IT friendly educational system (see the next part). They are quite missing these days as most of the current IT competitions focus on programming. In these competitions, members of our community can compete against each other in a friendly manner or form teams together which will be competing against other teams. 

Secondly, we can use the IT competitions (mostly programming) which exist today. Examples of already existing competitions: 

  • Mladý programátor: https://soutez.tib.cz/souteze/mlady-programator 
  • Matematická olympiáda, kategorie P: http://mo.mff.cuni.cz/p/  
  • High school programming competitions organised in many regions - example for Pardubice region: http://soutez.spse.cz/PRO-WAS/  

Our IT community should encourage its members to make teams with which they will participate in the existing competitions and also organise some prep courses for them. (for example, explanations of assignments from previous years which are available in some cases.) 

We consider it very important from the networking point of view that team building is present, apart from the educational part. Examples: in the weekend courses and summer schools, include parties in the evening (of course, only with non-alcoholic drinks as we aim for participants under 18 years of age), chatting by a campfire, playing games, LAN parties, celebrating birthdays together etc. Short networkings should be also included with the seminars and competitions. 

It is probable that some high school students decide not to study IT or related universities due to the fact that they believe it is a non-social place full of introverted people. Therefore, it is crucial that the community holds also after enrolling in university and that the high school students know it in advance. This way the above described fear will not be present and they will consider IT schools somewhat more acceptable. This can be achieved with having ambassadors (ideally students) of the community on each given faculty who are willing to help and advise the new students in case that the new students need it. A good way to start is to include the faculties which prg.ai is made of.  

Building an AI Friendly Education System

  • Enhancing Curriculae
  • Main Areas of Focus
  • Concrete Steps

The aim is to use modern digital technology more expediently and proficiently in learning and teaching, hence foster the highschoolers’ relationship to IT and AI in particular. The prg.ai campaign should strive to form a foundation that would ameliorate the schools' and teacher's methodological foundation. Parallel to Technology Foundation for Education which supports information and communication technology-related education development in Estonia. This should then result in: widening the students' horizons and their potential utilization and students actively becoming part of the evolution process (for instance, creating their own virtual reality tours).

Internet of Things: increasingly relevant technologies that, apart from being used in everyday life – an exciting (futuristic on the one hand, and close to life on the other) technology, are directly connected to other recurring technological fields (cloud computing, big data, AI, virtual and augmented reality, robotics).

Data analysis: current generations becoming comfortable with working with data, being presented with raw data and transforming them into spreadsheets and graphs, drawing conclusions based on their own visualizations

Gaming: robot programming helps children understand how technology works and gives them the opportunity to find new solutions and create programs themselves. Game-based learning is more likely to gain the attention of young learners as the engagement presented by social media and games is lacking in traditional learning methods.

1) Professional development for teachers

Workshops: transformation of education through new technologies: overview of how to implement them into the education process successfully 

Newsletters: written form of the aforementioned

Consultancy: providing technical support, tackling individual obstacles arising in the process of implementation (compatibility of devices used, ensuring the students' ability to update the software if necessary, tackling poor internet connection)

2) Universal platform

This platform would be distributed to schools of interest (grammar schools and particular specialized technical high schools). It would include separate topics under which the teachers could find suitable tools, lesson plans and other materials. The platform could, for instance, direct them to already existing applications and platforms. 

  • Anatomy 4D, an augmented reality application for learning human anatomy 
  • Google Expeditions, enables visiting of distant lands

The lesson plans would be prepared in collaboration with MA and PhD students of Digital Technology in Education. 

  • Dissertation work of BC Antonín Vlach: “Methodical materials for teaching robotics using Lego Mindstorms” 

3) Partnership with tech companies and universities

Institutions should ideally begin with the implementation of Internet of Things in physics and biology labs, ideally starting with the students' own smartphone sensors or cheap tools. Virtual reality can be used to simulate real work situations or simply enhance the learning process by enabling 3D adding labels to things of interest or enabling students to visualize discussed and abstract concepts (anatomy, solar system, etc.) based on the following concept:“You have to see for yourself to believe that something like that really exists”.

The partnership with tech companies and technically oriented institutions would ensure financial accessibility of the relevant technologies:

  • Smart Lab Kit: PocketLab Voyager, includes multiple sensors and shares lab results 
    on the student's own smartphone in real time
  • Body sensor set: heart rate and stress sensors, brain wave sensor
  • Room sensor set: smart home set with motion, temperature and other sensors and microcontrollers for network network experiments in the classroom
  • Virtual reality glasses
