Prague Golem

Prague has been always the epitome of dreams coming true, if obtained the right set of skills, knowledge and creativity. It is so deeply rooted in our culture that a historical folklore originating in 16th century has influenced numerous technical advancements up until today. The Golem of Prague tells the story of a rabbi who reportedly formed a golem figure out of clay collected in the Vltava river and brought it to life with a shem, in order to protect the Jewish community in Prague from anti-Semitic attacks and violent pogroms. One day however, the rabbi had forgotten to remove the shem and the golem has turned into a ruinous monster. Just upon succeeding to remove the shem from his mouth, the golem fell in pieces in front the well-known Prague synagogue, where it stays up until this day.

The importance of women




Not only is the diversity of opinions important to STEM, but so is the diversity in cultural and gender background. The importance of the rising presence of women in the technical and IT world is now more crucial than ever before, as the world is slowly transitioning from sometimes using technology to using technology on a daily basis. More women should therefore feel encouraged and confident that they have what it takes to offer their abilities and personal capabilities and help shape future generations towards safer and a more efficient usage of “man-made” technologies.
